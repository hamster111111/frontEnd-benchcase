Coaches & Champions: Reflecting on Juggle Jams 11-15

Paul Arneberg April 24, 2018 JUGHEADS Director Insights 8 Comments
The JUGHEADS team celebrating during Juggle Jam 14, May 2012

Our 2nd half of our 1st 20 JJs started with a bang, with JJ11 swelling to 140 members, including 12 seniors! With the addition of weekly paid coaches from among former and current Jugheads, our numbers grew along with our talents, abilities, and sense of company camaraderie.

The introduction of specialized coaching led to several breakthroughs in technical performance. While our Ultimate Club had always featured high-level juggling, our other clubs began closing the gap. The Thursday Rec. Club (later renamed Advanced Club) started producing complex club-passing patterns, while our Monday Rec and Elite Clubs incorporated more challenging choreographed sequences.

"The growth of JUGHEADS wasn't just in our numbers, but in the depth of character, leadership, and technical ability demonstrated by our youth. The passing of the torch to student leaders created a legacy that continues today." — Paul Arneberg, JUGHEADS Founder

The years 2008-2012 also saw our competitive presence grow nationally. Our teams and individuals regularly placed among the top finishers at the IJA (International Jugglers' Association) Youth Showcase and Teams Competitions. More importantly, our alumni began transitioning to coaching roles, creating a sustainable cycle of mentorship that enriched the program beyond measure.

JJ15 marked a special milestone as we celebrated 15 years of growth, with many of our original members returning as guest performers. The special JJ15 retrospective video highlighted just how far we'd come from our humble beginnings in 1994.
